üëâ Guidelines for recording Audioüìù

1. Choose a Quiet Environment:
Find a quiet space with minimal background noise. Avoid areas with traffic, appliances, or other sources of noise.

2. Minimize Echo:
Opt for rooms with soft furnishings like curtains, carpets, and furniture to reduce sound reflection and echo.

3. Avoid Filler Words:
Be conscious of using filler words like "um," "uh," "like," and "you know." Speak clearly and confidently.

4. Speak Slowly and Clearly:
Enunciate words clearly and avoid speaking too quickly. This helps in accurate transcription.

5. Position Microphone Correctly:
Position the microphone close to your mouth to captures your voice optimally while minimizing background noise.

6. Prevent Humming or Buzzing:
Ensure that electronic devices are properly grounded to avoid any humming or buzzing interference.
